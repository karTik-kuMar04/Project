# Research Sources for “India’s Forgotten Heroes”

This file is a source map for building a history website with solid evidence.  
Use it as a starting point for research, fact-checking, image sourcing, and bibliography building.

## Research rule for the site

For every major claim, try to keep:
- **1 primary source** (inscription, archive, museum record, excavation report, official site, government record)
- **1 secondary source** (scholarly book, journal article, university note, or museum interpretation)
- **1 visual proof** when possible (coin photo, map, inscription photo, artifact image)

If a claim cannot be supported by evidence, do not publish it.

---

## 1) Best source hierarchy

### Highest trust
- Government archaeological archives
- National museum collections
- National archives / public records
- UNESCO heritage pages
- Museum catalogs
- Peer-reviewed journal articles
- University repositories and textbooks

### Medium trust
- Reputed history websites with editorial review
- Academic encyclopedias
- University lecture notes / course material

### Low trust
- Random blogs
- Unattributed social media posts
- AI-generated history content
- Unsourced images on Google Images
- Wikipedia as a final source (okay only for initial lead generation, not for final publication)

## 2) Core websites you should use

### Archaeology, monuments, inscriptions, excavation evidence
1. **Archaeological Survey of India (ASI)**  
   https://asi.nic.in  
   Use for monuments, excavations, site history, inscriptions, and official archaeological information.

2. **UNESCO World Heritage Centre – India**  
   https://whc.unesco.org/en/statesparties/in  
   Use for officially recognized sites, heritage descriptions, and protected monuments.

3. **National Archives of India**  
   https://nationalarchives.nic.in  
   Use for public records, colonial-era documents, freedom struggle records, and historical archives.

4. **Indian Culture / Ministry of Culture portals**  
   https://indianculture.gov.in  
   Use for cultural heritage, manuscripts, art, and curated historical material.

### Coins, money, and numismatics
5. **National Museum, New Delhi**  
   https://www.nationalmuseumindia.gov.in  
   Use for coin galleries, artifact photos, collection records, and museum descriptions.

6. **IGNCA (Indira Gandhi National Centre for the Arts)**  
   https://ignca.gov.in  
   Use for art history, coins, architecture, manuscripts, and research PDFs.

7. **Reserve Bank of India Museum / RBI resources**  
   https://www.rbi.org.in  
   Use for currency history, money evolution, and coin-related institutional information.

### Maps and territorial history
8. **Perry-Castañeda Map Collection, University of Texas at Austin**  
   https://maps.lib.utexas.edu/maps/india.html  
   Use for historical maps of India, political boundaries, and archival map references.

9. **Library of Congress / other major map archives**  
   Use for older cartographic sources and historical map scans when needed.

### Freedom struggle and modern Indian history
10. **Gandhi Heritage Portal**  
    https://www.gandhiheritageportal.org  
    Use for Mahatma Gandhi’s writings, photos, chronology, and curated primary material.

11. **Prime Minister’s Office / PM India archive pages**  
    https://www.pmindia.gov.in  
    Use for official biographies, commemorative notes, and government-recognized historical summaries.

## 3) Best databases for research papers

1. **JSTOR**  
   https://www.jstor.org  
   Best for peer-reviewed journal articles on archaeology, coins, architecture, astronomy, and political history.

2. **Google Scholar**  
   https://scholar.google.com  
   Best for finding papers, citations, author names, and related academic work.

3. **NCERT textbooks and supporting material**  
   https://ncert.nic.in/textbook.php  
   Use for a clean, school-level but reliable historical overview and cross-checking basic chronology.

## 5) Starter research papers and academic sources

These are useful leads for deeper reading. Search each title in JSTOR or Google Scholar.

### Coins / numismatics
- **Notes on Gupta Coins** — E. J. Rapson (1891)  
  Good for early scholarly discussion of Gupta coinage.

- **The Origins of Coinage in Northern India** — T. R. Hardaker (1975)  
  Good for early coinage and monetization.

- **Source of Gold for Early Gold Coins of India** — U. Thakur (1980)  
  Good for economic and metallurgical questions around gold coinage.

- **The Hasnan Hoard of the Gupta Gold Coins** — D. K. Chakravarty (1977)  
  Good for hoard evidence and Gupta coin classification.

- **Treasures of the Gupta Empire: A Numismatic History** — recent scholarly work on Gupta coinage  
  Good for design, symbolism, trade, and chronology.

- **Stratigraphic Evidence of Coins in Indian Excavations** — S. C. Ray  
  Useful for understanding how coins are dated archaeologically.

### Astronomy / mathematics / science
- **Indian Astronomy** — D. Pingree (1978)  
  Good for historical astronomy context.

- **Scope and Development of Indian Astronomy** — S. R. Das (1936)  
  Good for a broad historical overview.

- **Modification of the Earlier Indian Planetary Theory** — K. Ramasubramanian (1994)  
  Good for technical history of planetary models.

- **The Purāṇas and Jyotiḥśāstra: Astronomy** — D. Pingree (1990)  
  Good for religious and astronomical traditions.

- **Vedic Astrology or Jyotirvigyan** — J. V. Narlikar (2001)  
  Useful for debate, terminology, and the distinction between astronomy and astrology.

### Political history / empire studies
- Academic works on the Maurya, Gupta, Chola, and Mughal periods
- University lecture PDFs and history department notes
- Peer-reviewed journal articles on administration, land grants, trade, and inscriptions

## 6) Reliable visual sources for your site

Use these for photos and illustrations, especially when you need proof:
- UNESCO site pages
- National Museum collection pages
- ASI monument pages
- Gandhi Heritage Portal photos and documents
- Museum collections from major institutions
- University map collections

Before publishing any image, check:
- Who owns the image
- Whether it is public domain / licensed
- Whether attribution is required
- Whether the caption matches the object, date, and site

## 7) Suggested website evidence format

For each card or article page, store:
- **Title**
- **Date / period**
- **Region**
- **What happened**
- **Why it matters**
- **Evidence type**
- **Source 1**
- **Source 2**
- **Image / map source**
- **Confidence level**
- **Notes**

Example:

**Topic:** Gupta coinage  
**Evidence:** Coin photos from museum collection + JSTOR article + hoard report  
**Confidence:** High

---

## 8) Important wording advice for your website

For sensitive or debated topics:
- Use **“foreign dynasty,” “campaign,” “rule,” “campaign of conquest,” “colonial power,”** or **“political transition”** instead of using a loaded label everywhere.
- For “invaders of India,” make sure each entry is historically specific and region-specific.
- Separate **fact**, **interpretation**, and **national memory**.
- When scholars disagree, say that clearly.

---

## 9) Practical workflow

1. Choose one topic.
2. Find 2–3 solid sources.
3. Collect at least one image or map with provenance.
4. Write a short factual summary.
5. Add citations in your database.
6. Only then write the website page.
